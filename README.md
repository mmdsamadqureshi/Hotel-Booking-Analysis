Hotel Booking Analysis
Project Overview

This project analyzes hotel booking data from an online travel platform to uncover customer booking behavior, cancellation patterns, revenue trends, and operational insights. The objective is to identify key business problems and provide data-driven recommendations to improve bookings and reduce cancellations.

Business Problem

Hotels frequently experience booking cancellations, revenue fluctuations, and uneven demand across booking channels. Understanding these patterns helps optimize pricing, improve occupancy, and enhance customer experience.

Key Insights
1. Web Channel Generated the Highest Bookings

The Web booking channel contributed the largest share of total bookings and revenue.

2. Travel Agent Bookings Showed Higher Cancellation

Travel Agent bookings experienced a significantly higher cancellation rate than other channels.

3. Standard Rooms Had Maximum Demand

Standard rooms accounted for the highest booking volume.

4. Four-Star Hotels Were Most Preferred

Most bookings were concentrated among 4-star hotels.

5. Strong Seasonal Trend

Booking volume increased during peak travel months, resulting in higher overall revenue.

Root Cause Analysis

Major reasons behind high cancellation rates include:

Long booking lead time
Specific booking channels
Lower customer commitment
Seasonal booking behavior
Business Recommendations
Introduce flexible pricing during low-demand periods.
Reduce cancellations through advance confirmation reminders.
Offer targeted promotions for underperforming channels.
Optimize pricing for high-demand room categories.
Improve marketing efforts during off-season months.
